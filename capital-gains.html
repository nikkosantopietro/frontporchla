⚡ Two things most homeowners get wrong
1. The $250K/$500K exclusion is powerful — but not unlimited. If you're single, the first $250,000 of gain is tax-free. Married filing jointly? $500,000. But gains above that threshold are taxable — and in California, state tax applies on top of federal.

2. A 1031 exchange doesn't apply to your primary residence. The 1031 exchange is for investment properties only. If you live in the home, you use the primary residence exclusion instead — not a 1031. Many homeowners assume they can roll gains into their next home tax-free. You can't — at least not the way a 1031 works.
