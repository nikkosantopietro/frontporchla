Understanding Your Home's Value
How It Works
Your estimate is a starting point,
not the final word.
Every month we pull your home's estimated value from ATTOM, one of the most trusted real estate data providers in the country. Here's exactly how that number is calculated — and why an in-person walkthrough tells the full story.
The Data Behind the Number
What ATTOM looks at to estimate your home's value
ATTOM's Automated Valuation Model (AVM) analyzes thousands of data points and recent sales in your area to produce a statistically-driven estimate of what your home is worth today.
🏡
Recent comparable sales
Homes similar in size, age, and style that have sold nearby in the last 3–6 months.
📐
Property characteristics
Square footage, lot size, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, year built, and property type.
📍
Location signals
Your specific neighborhood, street, school district, and proximity to amenities.
📈
Market trends
Price movement in your zone over the past 30, 60, and 90 days.
🏛️
Public records
Tax assessments, permit history, and recorded sale prices from the county recorder.
🔄
Updated monthly
Your estimate refreshes every month as new sales close and market conditions shift.
What the algorithm can't see
AVMs don't know about your kitchen remodel, the view from your back patio, the custom built-ins, or the condition of the roof. They work from public data — which means the estimate has a range, not a fixed number. That range is shown in every report we send you.
The Range Explained
Why we show you a low and a high
Every ATTOM estimate comes with a confidence range — a low and high value that reflects the natural uncertainty in automated modeling. A tighter range means strong comparable sales data nearby. A wider range means less data, which is common in neighborhoods with fewer transactions.

The midpoint is the best single estimate. The range tells you how much room there is for the real number to move — in either direction.
